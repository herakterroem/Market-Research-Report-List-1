<p><p>The molasses sugar market is categorized into three main types: light, dark, and blackstrap molasses. Light molasses is derived from the first boiling of sugar cane, offering a sweeter flavor and lighter color, popular for culinary uses. Dark molasses results from the second boiling, having a richer flavor and higher mineral content, often used in baking and cooking. Blackstrap molasses is the thickest and darkest, produced from the third boiling, known for its strong taste and high nutritional value, frequently used as a dietary supplement.</p></p>
