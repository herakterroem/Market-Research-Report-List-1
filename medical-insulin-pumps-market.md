<p><p>The medical insulin pumps market comprises two primary types: patch pumps and traditional pumps. Patch pumps are lightweight and adhesive, allowing for a discreet and portable delivery of insulin without external tubing, ideal for active users. In contrast, normal pumps feature a more complex design with tubing that connects to a cannula for insulin delivery. While traditional pumps offer robust features and greater user control, patch pumps emphasize convenience and ease of use, driving growth in the diabetes management sector.</p></p>
