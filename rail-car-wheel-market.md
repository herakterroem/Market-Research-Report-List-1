<p><p>The rail car wheel market primarily consists of two types: rolling steel wheels and cast steel wheels. Rolling steel wheels, typically produced through hot rolling processes, are known for their durability and resistance to wear, making them ideal for high-speed and heavy-load applications. In contrast, cast steel wheels are formed by pouring molten steel into molds, allowing for complex designs and weight distribution. Both types cater to different operational needs and environmental conditions in the rail transportation sector.</p></p>
