<p><p>The dietary modified starch market comprises various types, each serving unique functions in food applications. Phosphate starch enhances thickening and stability, while acetate starch improves texture and moisture retention. Oxidized starch acts as a thickening agent and emulsifier, optimizing food consistency. Acetylated distarch phosphate provides resistant starch properties, supporting digestive health. Other modified starches include dextrins and pregelatinized variants, used for their gelling, binding, and film-forming capabilities, catering to diverse dietary requirements and food processing needs.</p></p>
